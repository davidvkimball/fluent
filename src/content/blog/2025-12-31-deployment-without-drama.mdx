Every new client asks what tools they should use. After building marketing stacks for dozens of companies, here is what we actually recommend.

**Website:** Astro or Next.js with a headless CMS (CloudCannon, Sanity, or Contentful depending on team size). Static rendering for marketing pages, server rendering only where needed. Fast by default.

**Analytics:** GA4 with server-side tagging via Google Tag Manager server container. Supplement with PostHog or Amplitude for product analytics if you are SaaS.

**Email:** Klaviyo for e-commerce, Customer.io for SaaS. Both have excellent automation, solid deliverability, and integrations with everything else.

**Paid media:** Meta Ads Manager and Google Ads directly. Do not use a third-party platform to manage campaigns. The platforms themselves are the best tools. Use a spreadsheet or Supermetrics for cross-platform reporting.

**SEO:** Ahrefs for research and monitoring. Screaming Frog for technical audits. Google Search Console for performance data. That is the entire stack.

**CRM:** HubSpot for most B2B companies. Overpowered for small teams, but the free tier is generous and it scales.

**Project management:** Linear or Notion. Not both. Pick one and commit.

The best marketing stack is the one your team actually uses. Fancy tools with zero adoption are worse than spreadsheets with full adoption. Start simple, add tools when you have a specific problem they solve, and ruthlessly cut anything nobody touches.
