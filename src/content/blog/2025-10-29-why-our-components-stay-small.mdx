"We need to build brand awareness." I hear this in almost every initial client call. And almost every time, it is the wrong goal.

Not because brand does not matter. It does. But because "brand awareness" has become a catch-all excuse for marketing that cannot prove its value. An agency spends your budget on CPM-optimized video ads. Impressions go up. Reach goes up. Revenue stays flat.

Real brand awareness is not an ad format. It is the result of every interaction someone has with your company. Your website speed. Your email subject lines. Your customer support. Your product quality.

Start with performance campaigns optimized for conversions. You will build awareness as a byproduct of actually being in market. Invest in content that ranks in search. Measure direct traffic and branded search volume instead of impressions. Brand matters, but it is a result of doing everything else well. Not a line item on a media plan.
