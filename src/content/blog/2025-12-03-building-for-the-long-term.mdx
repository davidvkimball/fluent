Every year, someone declares SEO dead. Every year, organic search continues to drive more revenue than any other channel for the companies that do it well.

Yes, AI overviews are changing the SERP. Yes, zero-click searches are increasing. Yes, algorithm updates keep coming. None of this kills SEO. It kills bad SEO.

The companies panicking are the ones who built their organic strategy on thin content, keyword stuffing, and link schemes. They should be panicking. Google has been telegraphing this for years.

The companies thriving are the ones who invested in genuine expertise, comprehensive content, and technical foundations. Their traffic goes up after algorithm updates because they are exactly what Google is trying to surface.

Our approach: focus on topics, not keywords. Build topical authority by covering subjects comprehensively. Create content that humans genuinely want to read, share, and link to. Invest in technical SEO as infrastructure, not a one-time project. Measure outcomes in revenue and pipeline, not rankings.

SEO in 2026 is harder than it was in 2016. But it also rewards quality more than it ever has. That is a good trade if you are willing to do the work.
