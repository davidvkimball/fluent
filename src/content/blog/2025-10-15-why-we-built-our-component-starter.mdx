I ran marketing at two venture-backed startups before starting SIGNAL. At both, we hired agencies. At both, the experience was almost identical.

Month one: excitement. The kickoff call has twelve people on it. Strategies are presented in beautiful decks with stock photos of diverse teams high-fiving. You feel like you hired the right partner.

Month three: confusion. Reports arrive on time but nothing is moving. You ask hard questions in status calls and get word salad back. "We're optimizing the funnel." "The algorithm is still learning." "Brand awareness takes time."

Month six: frustration. You realize the "senior team" from the pitch is nowhere to be found. Your account is being managed by someone two years out of college who is juggling eight other clients. You are paying $15K/month for what a competent in-house hire could do for half the cost.

## The agency model is broken

Here is the uncomfortable truth: most agencies are structured to maximize revenue, not results. They sell retainers, not outcomes. Their incentive is to keep you paying, not to make you successful enough to bring it in-house.

This is not malice. It is structure. When your business model depends on retaining clients regardless of performance, you optimize for relationships over results. Nice dinners and quarterly business reviews replace hard conversations about what is actually working.

## What we do differently

When we started SIGNAL, we made three rules:

1. **No vanity metrics.** We report on revenue, not impressions. Pipeline, not reach. If we cannot tie it to money, we do not report it.
2. **Senior people do the work.** The person in the pitch is the person managing your account. We stay small on purpose.
3. **Honest timelines.** If something is not working at 90 days, we say so. We would rather lose a client by being honest than keep one by being vague.

Is this a good business model? Honestly, it is harder than the traditional approach. We cannot scale the way holding companies do. But our clients stay because the numbers justify it, not because switching is inconvenient.

That is the difference between noise and signal.
