Before starting SIGNAL, I hired and fired three agencies in four years. Each time, the pattern was the same. Great pitch, slow decline, expensive exit.

The first agency was a big name. They had impressive logos on their client page and a team of specialists. What they did not have was anyone who cared about our account. We were their smallest client by a factor of ten, and it showed.

The second was a boutique shop. They cared, but they did not have the depth. When we needed to scale paid media, they were out of their depth. When we needed technical SEO, they outsourced to a contractor who disappeared.

The third was the most frustrating. They were competent. They did good work. But they optimized for their metrics, not ours. Traffic was up. Leads were up. Revenue was flat. When I pressed for an explanation, they pointed at their dashboard and shrugged.

The lesson: agencies fail when their incentives do not align with yours. The best agencies tie their success to your success. The worst ones tie it to their contract length. Ask your agency what happens if the numbers do not move. If the answer involves more patience and more budget, you have your answer.
