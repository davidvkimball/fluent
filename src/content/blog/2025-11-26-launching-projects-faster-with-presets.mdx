We have built over 200 landing pages. After the first 50, we noticed something: the pages that convert best all follow the same structure. Not the same design, the same structure.

Hero section: one clear headline that states the outcome, not the product. Subtext that handles the primary objection. One CTA above the fold. That is it.

Social proof: immediately after the hero. Logos, testimonials, or a specific metric. People need permission to keep reading.

Problem agitation: two to three paragraphs that describe the pain your audience feels. Be specific. Generic pain gets generic attention.

Solution presentation: now you earn the right to talk about your product. Frame it as the bridge between their current state and the outcome from the headline.

Proof: case studies, screenshots, data. Show the receipts.

CTA: repeat the primary CTA with urgency or a risk reversal (money-back guarantee, free trial, no commitment).

FAQ: handle remaining objections. If someone scrolls this far, they are interested but not convinced.

This formula works for SaaS, e-commerce, services, and B2B. The copy changes. The images change. The structure stays the same because human psychology stays the same.
