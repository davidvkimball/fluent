Hiring an agency is a high-stakes decision. A good agency can transform your business. A bad one will burn six months and six figures before you realize the mistake.

Here are the questions we would ask if we were hiring ourselves.

**Who will actually do the work?** If the pitch team is all senior and the delivery team is all junior, that is a red flag. Ask to meet the people who will be in your account daily.

**What do your worst clients look like?** Any agency that says they have never had a bad engagement is lying. You want an agency that can articulate what goes wrong and what they learned from it.

**How do you report on results?** If the answer involves impressions, reach, or engagement rate as primary metrics, keep looking. You want revenue, pipeline, or at minimum, qualified leads.

**What happens if results are flat at 90 days?** Good agencies have a protocol for this. Bad agencies have excuses.

**Can I talk to a client you lost?** This is the nuclear question. If they can connect you with a former client who will speak honestly about the experience, that tells you more than any case study.

The best agencies are not afraid of hard questions. They welcome them because hard questions are how they win against competitors who rely on polish over substance.
