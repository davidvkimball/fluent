Eighty percent of the GA4 implementations we audit are broken. Not slightly off. Fundamentally misconfigured in ways that make every decision based on that data suspect.

The most common problems: enhanced measurement tracking phantom events, cross-domain tracking not configured, conversion events counting page views instead of actual conversions, and UTM parameters being stripped by redirects.

Here is what a proper GA4 setup looks like. First, disable enhanced measurement for everything except page views. Then set up custom events for the actions that actually matter: form submissions, purchases, demo requests. Use server-side tagging if you have any meaningful ad spend, because browser-side tracking loses 20-40% of conversions to ad blockers and ITP.

Set up proper cross-domain tracking if you use any subdomains or third-party checkout flows. Configure data streams correctly. And for the love of revenue, test your conversion tracking by actually converting on your own site and checking that the event fires correctly.

Bad data is worse than no data because it gives you confidence in wrong decisions. Fix your measurement layer before you spend another dollar on marketing.
